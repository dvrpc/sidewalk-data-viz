About:

Every street centerline across the 9-county DVRPC region was analyzed to find gaps in the sidewalk network. For each centerline:

  • the length of parallel sidewalks was identified
  • the sidewalk length was divided by the centerline's length
  • the resulting ratio was classified into one of three groups:

The walking time to the five nearest public transit stops was calculated for the start and end points of every sidewalk segment across the 9-county DVRPC region.

The number of minutes to the nearest stop is shown with each point's color. In some cases it is impossible to walk to any transit stops solely via the sidewalk network. These locations are shown with a red point.

For every rail station across the 9-county DVRPC region a set of one-mile "walksheds" was calculated, one using the sidewalk network and the other using the street centerline network. These two areas were compared as a ratio to highlight stations where sidewalk accessibility is limited or non-existent.

Clicking on a rail station will zoom in and show the two associated walksheds.
